What Your Body Burns At Rest

Your calorie floor — before you take a single step

Example: 5'7" · 160 lb · age 30 · BMR ≈ 1,478 cal/day

Mifflin-St Jeor equation
(10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) ± sex constant
01

What it measures

Calories for breathing, circulation, cell production, and body temperature — at complete rest

02

What it doesn't include

No walking, exercise, digestion, or daily movement — BMR is the floor, not your full day

03

What changes it over time

Weight, age, and muscle mass shift your resting burn — more lean mass generally means a higher BMR

04

Why it matters

Every calorie target starts here — eating far below BMR long-term can work against muscle retention
